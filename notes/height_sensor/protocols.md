### Citric acid making protocol
#### Materials:
- goggles
- gloves
- coat
- solid citric acid
- small powder holder
- fine-scale weight
- acid container
- acid waste collector
- water source

#### Procedure:
1. put coat, eye shield, gloves.
2. weigh 0.1g of citric acid.
3. weigh 1 liter of tap/deionized water
4. pour a small amount of water
5. put the acid
6. wash the rest of acid with water
7. seal the bottle and shake
8. pour the rest of the water
9. seal and shake
10. acid holder to garbage
11. make sure you left enough space in the bottle for pressure to not overbuild,
or othewise loosen the lid slightly.
12. mix on the shaker for an hour
13. take of the shaker, let it stand the night in the fume hood.
Might be slightly open.

### Anodization protocol
#### Materials:
- goggles
- gloves
- coat
- trough with tantalum wires mounted straight.
- power generator with power cable.
- black and red crocodile cables
- ampermeter
- male-male cables as needed
- acetone
- acid in container
- clean paper towel
- acid waste collector
- meter
- pliers or 1 plier and 1 melkhatzayim

#### Procedure:
1. Straighten the wires as much as possible.
2. Cut large enough pieces to fit nicely in the holder + exposed side.
3. Put goggles and gloves.
4. Clean everything with acetone and tap water. Also gloves.
5. Put the trough in the hood on a clean plate, preferably with a clean paper in between.
6. Put the power generator beside it but away, so that no acid spill can easily reach it.
7. Mount the wires in the holder.
8. Gently put the holder in place and close the screws.
9. Write formation voltage used.
10. Connect the power generator to socket, set max current on required mA limit value (can be more exact if power generator allows) and voltage on maximum.
11. Fill the trough with citric acid.
12. Connect the + to the tantalum and the - to the stainless steel. 
13. Connect a resisor in series.
14. Connect a voltmeter/raspberry pi voltage sensor on it.
15. Turn on the ports.
16. Write the time of start.
17. Write time of formation voltage.
18. Write time of minimum current.
19. (If we go for anodization sealing, fill a small amount of citric acid back into the trough and put the end of the cut wires in it, connect + and - and anodize up to formation voltage.)
20. Disconnect the circuit and carefully collect the + and - wires. If needed, clean them with a damp paper towel, then with a dry one. 
21. Disconnect the power generator from socket. If needed, clean cable and generator with damp and dry paper.
22. If anodization is done, put generator in place.
23. Prepare a clean sheet of paper for to put cleaned trough parts and wires to dry.
24. Spill the wasted acid to acid collector. Mark it if needed.
25. Disassemble the trough. Be careful with the anodized wires!
26. Clean the trough parts with tap/deionized water.
27. Store/pack acid container.
28. Return goggles, coat to place.

### Quick capacity check
#### Material:
- gloves
- a small cup
- a capacitance meter and +- wires
- anodized tantalum wire
- meter/ruler
- deionized/tap water source
#### Procedure:
1. Cut the anodized wire.
2. Cover the end with some temporary isolator.
3. Rinse the wire in deionized water.
4. Prepare a cup with tap water in it.
5. Connect anodized wire to +.
6. Connect an exposed wire to -.
7. If tips are not isolated, make sure the multimeter works at voltage below 1V, so that the natural oxide at the tip can serve as a (partial) isolation.
8. Formation voltage vs. capacitance:
   1. 60V -> ~ (2.0-6.8)[nF/mm]
   2. 80V -> ~ (1.5-5.1)[nF/mm]
   3. 120V -> ~ (1.0-3.4)[nF/mm] (!!!Avoid because of risk of scintillation!!!)
